About Me

Michael got a Nintendo 64 when he was a child, and has been passionate about games ever since. When he found out that people could actually make these game, he decided he wanted to be part of the game development community. He started by going to Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and received a degree in Game Development and Electronic Arts along with a Computer Science minor. From there, he was motivated to work on new and interesting ideas, whether it is for work such as at Muzzy Lane Software, an educational video game company, or his own personal works such as Shock Jocks.

Moments of Silence

This was my Art Thesis project for senior year of college. It is a pair of hands animating a poem in sign language about the different emotions silence can represent.

Rail Brawler

Rail Brawler is another project I did for my senior year of collage. We made a game were you gain powers based off of how you posed. For example, flexing makes your attacks stronger, while stretching lets you attack multiple enemies at once.

Roles: Designer, Artist
